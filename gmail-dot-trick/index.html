What is Gmail Dot Trick?

The Gmail Dot Trick is a clever feature within Google's Gmail service that allows users to create virtually unlimited variations of their primary email address, all of which still deliver to the same inbox. Google simply ignores periods (dots) in the username portion of a Gmail address. For example, emails sent to yourname@gmail.com, your.name@gmail.com, and y.o.u.r.n.a.m.e@gmail.com will all arrive in the same yourname@gmail.com inbox.

This trick is incredibly useful for a variety of purposes, from filtering incoming emails, signing up for multiple accounts on websites that require unique email addresses, or even identifying which services might be selling your email address if you use a specific dotted alias for each signup.
